\begin{abstract}
  Real time blood glucose monitoring is crucial for individuals. Either hyperglycemia or hypoglycemia yields potential risks on personal heath.
  Nevertheless, high-frequent and non-intrusive monitoring of blood glucose concentration is difficult and even impractical in our daily life.
  Whether the traditional finger prick test or the advanced continuous blood glucose monitor (CGM) suffers from all manner of real-life interference.
  To this end, we present \sysname, a mobile service that provides personalized real-time tracking of blood glucose.  It invokes the embedded sensors (\ie
  acceleromenter, microphone and light sensor) of smartphones to sense sleep quality and calculate the daily calory consumption automatically, and then
  infers the current blood glucose level with the combination of the intakes of food, drug and insulin by a novel and effective statistical model (\modelname).
  Distinct with the existing predicted approaches, \modelname takes advantages of multi-task learning to capture the individual similarities
  but preserve their personality, and also makes full use of deep dynamic recurrent neural network (RNN) to describe the temporal fluctuation of blood glucose.
  By encoding the RNN model into the framework of multi-task learning, \modelname not only establishes the relationship of external factors and blood glucose
  level, but also transfers this correlations between users efficiently. Based on the inference of \modelname, \sysname tracks the
  blood glucose level every 3 minutes.
  The extensive experimental results demonstrate that \modelname enable to remedy the sparsity of individual data and improves the inference results, guaranteeing
  \sysname tracking blood glucose efficiently.
\end{abstract}
